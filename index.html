Vitamin D

Why It Matters
Vitamin D regulates calcium absorption, supports immune function, and influences mood. Deficiency is linked to weakened bones, depression, and increased infection risk.
Peak Production: Midday
UVB rays are strongest between 11 AM–1 PM. Your skin synthesizes vitamin D fastest during peak sun angle. Just 20–30 minutes of midday sun can produce 10,000+ IU.
Cholesterol Boosts Absorption
Vitamin D is fat-soluble. Eating cholesterol-rich foods (eggs, salmon, grass-fed butter) before sun exposure increases VD absorption by 35–50%. Your body needs dietary fat to use the vitamin it produces.
Clothing Matters
More exposed skin = faster production. Swimwear or minimal clothing accelerates vitamin D synthesis. UVB doesn't penetrate clothing, so bare arms & legs are key.
Avoid Sunscreen for VD
SPF blocks 93–98% of UVB rays. If your goal is vitamin D synthesis, skip sunscreen for the first 20 minutes. You can apply it after to prevent burn damage.
💨

Nitric Oxide

Why It Matters
NO is a signaling molecule that relaxes blood vessels, improving blood flow, lowering blood pressure, and enhancing oxygen delivery to muscles. Better NO = better cardio health & athletic performance.
Available All Day
Unlike vitamin D, nitric oxide production from UVA rays doesn't peak at midday. You can optimize NO production throughout the day—morning, afternoon, or evening sun all contribute equally.
Movement Multiplies It
Activity increases NO production by 10–28%. Walking, running, or exercising in the sun creates more NO than sitting still. Combine sun exposure with movement for maximum cardiovascular benefit.
Better Workouts
Higher NO = more oxygen to muscles, faster recovery, and better endurance. Training in the sun (even light walking) boosts performance compared to indoor exercise.
🧠

Serotonin

Why It Matters
Serotonin is your mood molecule. Higher levels boost happiness, reduce anxiety & depression, improve focus, and enhance motivation. It's why sunny days feel better than cloudy ones.
Morning Light = Better Sleep
Morning sun (6–8 AM) resets your circadian rhythm, boosting serotonin during the day and melatonin at night. Just 10–15 minutes of outdoor light improves sleep quality & mood for the entire day.
Evening Light Matters Too
Afternoon/evening sun (4–7 PM) maintains serotonin levels and prepares your body for sleep by triggering melatonin production at the right time. This improves sleep onset & depth.
Movement Amplifies Effects
Combining sun exposure with activity (walking, hiking, sports) increases serotonin production by 15–20%. A morning walk in the sun is one of the best mood & sleep optimizers.
Clothing is Irrelevant
Serotonin responds to light entering your eyes, not skin exposure. You benefit from serotonin production even in long sleeves—just make sure your eyes are exposed to outdoor light.
🎯

Optimization Tips

The 3-Window Protocol
Morning (6:30–7:30 AM): 10–20 min for mood & sleep sync. Midday (12–1 PM): 20–40 min for vitamin D. Evening (6:30–7:30 PM): 10–30 min for sleep prep & serotonin.
Heating Matters
Warm skin increases blood flow and nutrient absorption. Saunas or hot showers before sun exposure can amplify vitamin D and NO production. The heat opens capillaries, making absorption more efficient.
Consistency Beats Intensity
Daily 20-minute sessions outperform occasional 2-hour sessions. Your body adapts best to regular, moderate sun exposure rather than sporadic intense exposure.
Track Your Progress
Use SunBio to monitor your daily nutrient totals. Aim for consistent targets: 2,000+ IU VD, 20+ μmol NO, 50%+ serotonin daily for optimal health.
